Prisoner's Dilemma
Cooperation vs. Self-Interest
▾
Two players each choose to cooperate or defect. If both cooperate — both win. If one defects — they take all. If both defect — both lose. Repeated play changes everything.
💡 Apply to Life & Business
Defectors win in one-shot games but lose in repeated play. Your reputation is your strategy. Be the first mover in cooperation — then mirror the other's behaviour (Tit-for-Tat).
Action Steps
1. Identify 3 key relationships where you've been "defecting" unconsciously
2. Make a cooperative first move — share value without expectation
3. Set clear reciprocity expectations with key collaborators
⚖️
Nash Equilibrium
Finding the Stable Winning Position
▾
A state where no player improves by changing their strategy unilaterally. Both are in their best response to each other. The stable point nobody wants to leave.
💡 Apply to Career & Wealth
Build a niche moat — a unique combination of skills, reputation, and assets that's not worth competing against. Position yourself where imitating you costs more than it returns.
1. Map your unique skill combination — what 3 things do very few combine?
2. Identify your "competitors" and what strategy they cannot counter
3. Reach the intersection where imitating you is not worth the cost
♾️
The Infinite Game
Play to Stay in the Game, Not to Win
▾
Finite games have fixed rules and a defined winner. Infinite games have changing rules and the goal is to keep playing. Life, business, and relationships are infinite games. Players who treat them as finite — trying to "win" — eventually run out of will or resources.
💡 Apply to Vision & Legacy
Stop comparing your Chapter 3 to someone else's Chapter 20. Your goal is to remain in the game long enough for compound interest — of skills, relationships, and capital — to work. Resilience beats intensity. Staying beats winning.
1. Write your "Just Cause" — the future you're building towards that's bigger than any one goal
2. Identify one finite game you're playing that's draining you — reframe it as infinite
3. Prioritise staying power — sleep, relationships, and sustainability over hustle
📡
Signalling Theory
What Your Actions Communicate
▾
In markets with information asymmetry, credible signals convey quality. Expensive signals work precisely because they're costly — they're hard to fake. A degree signals work ethic. A premium price signals quality. A public commitment signals conviction.
💡 Apply to Personal Brand
Consistency is your most credible signal. Showing up daily — even imperfectly — sends a message that's nearly impossible to fake: you're serious. Use your daily actions as deliberate signals to your market, your team, and yourself.
1. Audit what your current actions signal to others — does it match your intended brand?
2. Choose one costly, credible signal to start this month (public commitment, consistent output)
3. Remove false signals — stop looking busy, start being productive
💎
Antifragility
Get Stronger Under Stress
▾
Nassim Taleb's concept: some systems gain from disorder. Fragile things break under stress. Robust things survive. Antifragile things improve. Muscles, immune systems, great businesses — all antifragile. They need the stress to grow.
💡 Apply to Resilience & Wealth
Stop trying to eliminate all volatility. Build systems that extract upside from chaos: multiple income streams, skills that compound, networks that deepen during crises. The market crash is not your enemy — it's your filter.
1. Identify your "fragile" dependencies — single income, single skill, single platform
2. Add optionality — small bets in multiple directions with capped downside
3. Reframe setbacks — ask "how does this make me stronger?" after every hit
📈
Compound Effect Strategy
Small Edges × Time = Massive Advantage
▾
1% better every day = 37× better in a year. Compounding applies to wealth, skills, relationships, and reputation. The gains are invisible at first — all the growth clusters in the final phase. Most people quit too early.
💡 Apply to Your Vision Hierarchy
Your Tier 1 vision is the compound interest destination. Your Tier 3 daily actions are the deposits. Miss the deposit — miss the compound. Protect your streak of daily intentional actions above almost everything else.
1. Define your "1% daily action" — the smallest daily deed that moves your vision forward
2. Track a 90-day streak — consistency is the compound rate
3. Never miss twice — one miss is an accident, two is the start of a new habit
